Mercury

Mercury, the smallest planet in our solar system, is closest to the Sun, orbiting it every 88 Earth days. Its diameter is about 4,880 kilometers, making it only slightly larger than Earth's Moon. Mercury has a rocky, heavily cratered surface, with temperatures ranging from extremely hot (up to 430°C) during the day to extremely cold (down to -180°C) at night.
The planet lacks a significant atmosphere to retain heat. Mercury has a large iron core, which generates a weak magnetic field, about 1% as strong as Earth's. There is no water on Mercury's surface, but ice exists in permanently shadowed craters at its poles.
The planet's proximity to the Sun and rapid orbital speed make it challenging to observe from Earth. Despite its harsh conditions, missions like NASA's MESSENGER have provided valuable insights into Mercury's composition and geological history.
